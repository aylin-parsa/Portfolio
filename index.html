About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Marketing (Consumer Behavior) at Washington State University, expecting to graduate in December 2027. My dissertation research examines how involuntary relocation and cumulative experiences of discrimination act as psychological threats that reshape food consumption, decision-making, and everyday marketplace behavior among vulnerable populations.

Before moving into academic research, I spent a decade in industry across marketing management, market research, and entrepreneurship in Iran — including founding a travel and itinerary services company and leading marketing strategy for an industrial manufacturing distributor. That background grounds my research in real consumer and organizational behavior rather than theory alone.

I hold an M.S. in Business Administration – Marketing from Tehran Science and Research Azad University and a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from Azad University, Iran. I am bilingual in Persian and English.

Research focus

Forced relocation

How involuntary moves generate social identity threat, and how that threat is expressed through disrupted dietary behavior across the food processing continuum.

Cumulative discrimination

A Cumulative Vulnerability framework testing how sustained exposure to group-based discrimination undermines healthy consumption through emotional distress and effort aversion.

Retail & shopping geography

How basket size, local assortment, and shopping-goal complexity shape where and how consumers consolidate their purchases across markets.
